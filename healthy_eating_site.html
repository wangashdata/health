Diabetes

Diabetes is a condition where blood sugar levels are too high due to insulin issues. A healthy diet helps control sugar levels and prevent complications.

Recommended Diet

Focus on whole grains, leafy greens, lean proteins, and low-sugar fruits.

Sample Recipe: Quinoa Salad

  • 1 cup cooked quinoa
  • 1/2 cup cherry tomatoes
  • 1/2 cup cucumber
  • 1 tbsp olive oil & lemon juice

Mix all ingredients and serve chilled.

Hypertension (High Blood Pressure)

Hypertension increases the risk of heart disease and stroke. The DASH diet is recommended.

Recommended Diet

Eat fruits, vegetables, low-fat dairy, and reduce salt intake.

Sample Recipe: Steamed Veggie Bowl

  • 1 cup broccoli
  • 1 cup carrots
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • A pinch of herbs instead of salt

Steam vegetables, drizzle with olive oil, and season with herbs.

Heart Disease

Heart disease is caused by plaque buildup in arteries. A heart-healthy diet helps reduce risks.

Recommended Diet

Include omega-3 rich fish, nuts, whole grains, and avoid trans fats.

Sample Recipe: Grilled Salmon

  • 1 salmon fillet
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • Lemon juice, garlic, pepper

Marinate salmon and grill for 10 minutes until cooked through.

Celiac Disease

Celiac disease is an autoimmune disorder where gluten damages the small intestine.

Recommended Diet

Gluten-free whole foods such as rice, corn, potatoes, and gluten-free flour.

Sample Recipe: Gluten-Free Pancakes

  • 1 cup gluten-free flour
  • 1 egg
  • 1 cup milk (or almond milk)
  • 1 tsp baking powder

Mix ingredients, cook on a skillet, and serve warm.

Conclusion

Healthy eating is the foundation of preventing and managing chronic diseases. Choose natural foods, cook with love, and eat mindfully for a healthier future.
